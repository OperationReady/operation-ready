Six paths covering the full pre-deployment knowledge base — from maritime fundamentals to survivor interaction and mental health resilience.

Path 1 6 modules
Maritime fundamentals
How ships work, vessel orientation, weather and sea state, navigation, personal safety, and watch systems.
Start here if you've never been to sea
Path 2 7 modules
SAR operations
The SAR system, search patterns, RHIB operations, survivor recovery, mass rescue, scenario simulator, and documentation.
Includes interactive scenario simulator
Path 3 5 modules
Medical & first response
Triage at sea, drowning and water rescue medicine, hypothermia, medical emergencies, and psychological first aid.
For medical staff and first responders
Path 4 5 modules
Survivor interaction
Who the survivors are, first contact, working with interpreters, vulnerable persons, and humanitarian principles.
Essential for every crew member
Path 5 4 modules
Mental health & resilience
What to expect emotionally, vicarious trauma, managing difficult operations, and fatigue on deployment.
Often the most important path
Path 6 4 modules
Legal & humanitarian framework
Law of the sea, how the SAR system works legally, NGO operating frameworks, and incident documentation.
The framework behind the work
