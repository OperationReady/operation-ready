About Operation Ready

The gap between
briefing and boarding.

Operation Ready exists to close the preparation gap — for anyone going somewhere unfamiliar, demanding, and high-stakes.

The problem

Unprepared people
cost everyone time.

Every year, thousands of people join operational deployments — on vessels, at research stations, in the field — without the foundational knowledge to function effectively from day one. They don't know the environment. They don't know the language. They don't know what's expected of them.

The people around them absorb the cost. Experienced crew slow down to explain things that should already be known. Operations become less safe. Deployments become less effective.

This is not a talent problem. It's a preparation problem. And it's almost entirely solvable with the right resources, available before people arrive.

How we work

Built on three principles

Practitioner-built
Every module is written by someone with direct operational experience in that context — not by instructional designers working from textbooks. The content reflects what actually matters on day one, not what looks comprehensive on a syllabus.
Before you arrive
The preparation happens before deployment, not during it. Modules are designed to be completed in the weeks before departure — giving people the foundational knowledge that makes onboard induction faster and more effective for everyone.
Accessible by design
No accounts, no downloads, no institutional login required. A link is enough. The platform works on any device, in any browser, in multiple languages. The goal is zero friction between a person and the preparation they need.
Who built this

Written from
the deck.

Operation Ready was built by a maritime professional with direct operational experience across humanitarian search and rescue in the Central Mediterranean, conservation vessel operations, and sub-Antarctic remote station work.

I've been there as new crew — on conservation vessels, SAR vessels, and remote stations. I know what it feels like to arrive underprepared, and what it costs the people around you when you do. This is exactly what I would have wanted before stepping aboard.

The platform is continuously maintained as operational practice evolves. Each vertical is built in collaboration with people who are actively doing the work — not working from a distance.
